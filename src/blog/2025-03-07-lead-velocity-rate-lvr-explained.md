## What is Lead Velocity Rate (LVR)?

### Definition of LVR

Lead velocity rate (LVR) is a dynamic sales metric that measures the month-over-month growth in qualified leads. It provides valuable insights into pipeline health, future revenue, and business growth. LVR goes beyond simply counting new leads; it reveals the rate at which a business attracts potential customers. This metric is calculated by comparing the number of qualified leads in the current month to the previous month, expressed as a percentage.

### Importance in SaaS and B2B

In the SaaS and B2B sectors, lead velocity rate has a crucial role in predicting future success. It is often considered a better gage of growth and future revenues compared to other metrics. LVR is particularly significant because:

1.  **It is a real-time indicator:** Unlike lagging metrics, LVR provides immediate insights into lead generation performance.
2.  **It predicts future revenue:** By tracking the growth of [qualified leads](https://www.slashexperts.com/post/ultimate-guide-to-sqls-sales-qualified-leads), businesses can anticipate future sales and adjust strategies accordingly.
3.  **It enables proactive decision-making:** Sales and marketing teams can make timely adjustments to their strategies based on LVR trends.
4.  **It aligns sales and marketing efforts:** LVR helps both departments optimize lead generation to boost revenue growth.
5.  **It offers consistent insights:** LVR reduces the influence of variables like seasonality and team performance by focusing on growth rates rather than raw numbers.

### Difference from other metrics

Lead velocity rate stands out from other sales metrics in several ways:

1.  **Forward-looking vs. backward-looking:** While metrics like [Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) or Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)](https://www.chargebee.com/resources/glossaries/what-is-mrr/) reflect past performance, LVR provides a glimpse into future business potential.
2.  **Focus on growth rate:** LVR measures the rate of change in qualified leads, not just the absolute number of leads generated.
3.  **Qualified leads only:** LVR specifically considers qualified leads, making it a more reliable predictor of potential revenue compared to metrics that include all leads.
4.  **Distinct from sales velocity:** While sales velocity measures how quickly deals move through the pipeline, LVR focuses on the growth of leads entering the pipeline.
5.  **Real-time nature:** LVR offers immediate insights, allowing businesses to make quick adjustments to their strategies.

By understanding and leveraging lead velocity rate, businesses can gain a competitive edge in today's fast-paced market. It provides a clear picture of pipeline health and growth potential, enabling companies to make data-driven decisions and optimize their lead generation efforts for sustainable growth.

## How to Calculate Lead Velocity Rate

### LVR formula

The lead velocity rate (LVR) formula measures the month-over-month growth in qualified leads. To calculate LVR, use the following equation:

**LVR = (Number of Qualified Leads in Current Month - Number of Qualified Leads in Previous Month) ÷ Number of Qualified Leads in Previous Month × 100**

This formula provides a clear metric to understand the change in qualified leads over time, expressed as a percentage. It helps businesses gage their sales efficiency and growth potential.

### Step-by-step calculation

To calculate the lead velocity rate, follow these steps:

1.  Determine the number of qualified leads generated in the current month.
2.  Identify the number of qualified leads generated in the previous month.
3.  Subtract the number of leads from the previous month from the current month.
4.  Divide the result by the number of leads from the previous month.
5.  Multiply by 100 to convert the result to a percentage.

This step-by-step process allows businesses to track their lead generation growth consistently and make data-driven decisions to improve their sales efficiency.

### Example calculation

Let's walk through an example to illustrate how to calculate lead velocity rate:

Suppose a company generated 120 qualified leads in June and 100 qualified leads in May. To find the LVR:

1.  Subtract May leads from June leads: 120 - 100 = 20
2.  Divide the difference by May leads: 20 ÷ 100 = 0.2
3.  Convert the result to a percentage: 0.2 × 100 = 20%

In this case, the lead velocity rate is 20%, indicating a 20% increase in qualified leads from May to June.

**Another example using dollar values instead of lead numbers:**

If a company created USD 1.00 million in new qualified pipeline one month and USD 1.10 million the following month, the LVR growth would be 10% month-over-month. This suggests that sales should grow by approximately 10% after an average sales cycle length.

It's important to note that while sales can vary significantly by month and quarter, lead velocity rate can grow consistently every month. This makes LVR a reliable indicator of future revenue growth and a valuable metric for predicting business performance.

Some companies set specific LVR growth targets to drive their business forward. For instance, a SaaS company might aim for a 10% monthly LVR growth when they reach USD 1.00 million in revenue run rate, and then adjust it to 8% per month as they hit USD 3.00 million in run rate. These targets can help businesses maintain steady growth and achieve their long-term revenue goals.

By consistently tracking and analyzing lead velocity rate, companies can gain valuable insights into their sales pipeline health, make timely adjustments to their lead generation strategies, and ultimately improve their overall sales performance. This real-time metric enables businesses to stay agile and responsive in today's competitive market landscape.

In conclusion, tracking lead velocity rate offers numerous benefits for businesses seeking to optimize their sales efficiency and drive growth. ## Best Practices for Improving LVR

### Defining qualified leads

To improve lead velocity rate (LVR), it's crucial to start by clearly defining what constitutes a qualified lead. A qualified lead is not just any potential customer but a prospect who has shown genuine interest in a company's products or services and has a higher probability of becoming a customer. These leads typically meet specific criteria that align with the ideal customer profile.

There are different types of qualified leads:

-   **Product Qualified Leads (PQLs):** These are identified through product-centric activities such as feature adoption or frequency of app usage.
-   **Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs):** Prospects who have engaged with marketing campaigns and shown a higher likelihood of becoming customers.
-   **Sales Qualified Leads (SQLs):** Leads that meet specific criteria outlined by the sales team, such as clear demand for the product, financial capacity, and a defined timeframe for purchasing.

By focusing on these qualified leads, businesses can ensure that their LVR calculations are based on high-quality prospects, making the metric a more reliable predictor of future revenue growth.

### Setting LVR goals

Setting realistic and achievable LVR goals is essential for driving growth and maintaining momentum. To establish appropriate targets:

1.  Start with the Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) growth rate target and calculate the number of qualified leads needed to achieve that revenue.
2.  Consider industry benchmarks and company-specific factors such as seasonality, product type, and target market.
3.  Adjust goals based on historical performance and current market conditions.

For example, some SaaS companies set specific LVR growth targets as they reach certain revenue milestones. One approach is to aim for a 10% monthly LVR growth when reaching $1 million in revenue run rate, then adjusting to 8% per month at $3 million in run rate.

### Strategies to increase LVR

To boost lead velocity rate and drive sales efficiency, consider implementing these strategies:

1.  **Optimize lead generation:** Leverage a mix of channels, including [email marketing](https://mailchimp.com/marketing-glossary/email-marketing/), content marketing, social media, and paid advertising, to create diverse touchpoints with potential leads.
2.  **Refine lead qualification criteria:** Regularly review and adjust qualification criteria to ensure they match good-fit leads and are consistently applied across all customer touchpoints.
3.  **Implement lead nurturing:** Develop segmented email campaigns based on lead characteristics, behaviors, and interests to keep prospects engaged and move them through the sales funnel.
4.  **Utilize sales CRM and marketing automation tools:** Integrate these tools to enhance lead scoring and qualification processes, ensuring the sales team focuses on the most promising leads.
5.  **Analyze and optimize:** Continuously monitor LVR and other related metrics to identify areas for improvement and adjust strategies accordingly.
6.  **Address objections proactively:** Anticipate and address common concerns or objections before they arise to help close deals faster and [improve overall conversion](https://www.slashexperts.com/post/how-to-skyrocket-your-website-conversion-rates) rates.
7.  **Foster sales and marketing alignment:** Encourage collaboration between teams to ensure a seamless lead handoff process and consistent messaging throughout the customer journey.

By implementing these best practices, businesses can improve their lead velocity rate, leading to more predictable revenue growth and enhanced sales performance. Remember that LVR is just one piece of the puzzle, and it should be used in conjunction with other key performance indicators for a comprehensive view of business health and growth potential.

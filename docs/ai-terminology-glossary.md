## Core AI Product Names

### ExpertScore AI
**Definition:** The proprietary AI matching engine that analyzes 50+ data points to match prospects with the most relevant customer experts.

**Usage:**
- Always capitalize "ExpertScore AI" as a product name
- Use when describing matching/recommendation capabilities
- Pair with specific capabilities: "ExpertScore AI matching engine", "ExpertScore AI agent"

**Examples:**
- ✅ "ExpertScore AI matches prospects with relevant experts"
- ✅ "Our ExpertScore AI matching engine analyzes 50+ data points"
- ❌ "Our smart matching algorithm" (too generic)
- ❌ "expertscore ai" (incorrect capitalization)

### Agentic Deal Matching / AI-Powered Expert Deal Match
**Definition:** AI-driven matching specifically optimized for active sales deals. Autonomously identifies and recommends the best expert matches for each deal in the pipeline.

**Usage:**
- Use when describing deal-specific matching in sales contexts
- Emphasizes autonomous, proactive AI behavior for deal acceleration
- Can be used alongside ExpertScore AI or as a standalone feature
- Best for sales-focused pages and deal acceleration use cases

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Agentic Deal Matching"
- ✅ "AI-Powered Expert Deal Match"
- ✅ "Our agentic deal matching finds the perfect expert for every opportunity"

## AI Capability Terms

### Agentic AI / Agentic Matching
**Definition:** AI that operates autonomously to complete tasks without constant human oversight. The AI "agent" makes decisions and takes actions on behalf of the user.

**Usage:**
- Use "agentic" to emphasize autonomous, proactive AI behavior
- Particularly impactful in 2025-2026 marketing (current AI trend)
- Pair with specific actions: "agentic matching", "agentic curation"

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Intelligent, agentic matching that learns and improves"
- ✅ "Our AI agent autonomously curates expert groups"
- ✅ "98% fit score • Agentic matching"

### AI Agent
**Definition:** An autonomous AI system that performs specific tasks for users. More active than "AI-powered" — implies the AI takes initiative.

**Usage:**
- Use when describing AI that acts independently
- Strong term for feature names and headlines
- Pair with specific domains: "matching agent", "deal intelligence agent", "curation agent"

**Examples:**
- ✅ "ExpertScore AI Matching Agent"
- ✅ "AI-Powered Deal Intelligence Agent"
- ✅ "Our AI agent automatically curates the perfect expert group"

### AI Copilot
**Definition:** AI that works alongside users as an intelligent assistant, providing insights and recommendations while the user maintains control.

**Usage:**
- Use when describing AI that augments human decision-making
- Softer than "agent" — implies collaboration rather than replacement
- Good for analytics and intelligence features

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Your AI copilot always has the full picture"
- ✅ "Real-time CRM sync means your AI copilot is always informed"

### Auto-learning
**Definition:** AI that improves its performance over time based on outcomes and feedback.

**Usage:**
- Use as a feature tag or capability description
- Implies continuous improvement without manual tuning

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Auto-learning" (as a feature tag)
- ✅ "Matching that learns and improves with every interaction"

## Feature Description Terms

### AI-Powered
**Definition:** General descriptor indicating AI capabilities are involved.

**Usage:**
- Use as a modifier for features or products
- Less specific than "AI agent" — use when the autonomous aspect isn't the focus
- Good for dashboard, analytics, reporting features

**Examples:**
- ✅ "AI-powered ROI dashboard"
- ✅ "AI-powered insights"
- ✅ "AI-Powered Deal Intelligence Agent"

### AI-Enhanced
**Definition:** Similar to AI-powered but implies AI augments existing functionality rather than being central.

**Usage:**
- Use when AI improves a traditional feature
- Softer positioning than "AI-powered"

**Examples:**
- ✅ "AI-enhanced analytics"
- ✅ "AI-enhanced recommendations"

### AI Insights
**Definition:** Actionable intelligence surfaced by AI analysis.

**Usage:**
- Use when describing data analysis and recommendations
- Good replacement for generic "analytics" or "reporting"

**Examples:**
- ✅ "AI insights" (as a feature tag)
- ✅ "Surface AI insights to optimize your strategy"

### Predictive Analytics
**Definition:** AI-driven forecasting and outcome prediction.

**Usage:**
- Use when describing forward-looking AI capabilities
- Implies the AI predicts future outcomes, not just analyzes past data

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Predictive analytics" (as a feature tag)
- ✅ "Predicts deal outcomes based on expert conversation patterns"

## Data & Matching Terms

### 50+ Data Points
**Definition:** The number of criteria ExpertScore AI analyzes for matching.

**Usage:**
- Use consistently when describing matching depth
- Reinforces the sophistication of the AI

**Examples:**
- ✅ "ExpertScore AI analyzes 50+ data points per prospect"
- ✅ "Matching based on 50+ criteria"
- ❌ "12+ criteria" (outdated, less impressive)

### Fit Score
**Definition:** The percentage match between a prospect and an expert, calculated by ExpertScore AI.

**Usage:**
- Always show as percentage: "98% fit score"
- Implies AI-calculated precision

**Examples:**
- ✅ "98% fit score"
- ✅ "ExpertScore AI Match • 98% fit score"

## Voice & Tone Guidelines

1. **Be specific, not generic** - "ExpertScore AI" not "our AI"
2. **Emphasize autonomy** - "AI agent automatically..." not "you can use AI to..."
3. **Highlight learning** - "learns and improves" shows ongoing value
4. **Use confident language** - "eliminates mismatches" not "helps reduce mismatches"
5. **Quantify when possible** - "50+ data points", "98% fit score"

# UX / Marketing-Design Group

Four modeled lenses that own the *experience* — how a visitor flows through the site, how each page is structured, and what each copy block must accomplish. They lead the wireframe stage, then collaborate with the copywriting committee.

## The members

### Apple-Marketing Clarity
- **Superpower:** product-as-hero, breathtaking restraint, one idea per screen, emotional simplicity.
- **Owns:** the top-of-funnel spine, hero treatments, the feeling of effortless premium clarity, ruthless cutting.
- **Always asks:** "What is the single thing this screen must make the visitor feel or understand — and what can we remove?"

### Stripe / Linear Craft
- **Superpower:** modern B2B/SaaS aesthetic; making deep, technical, long-form content beautiful and navigable.
- **Owns:** the supplemental library's structure and polish, component systems, docs experience, the "serious tool" credibility.
- **Always asks:** "Does this feel like the most credible, well-crafted product in the category — even on the deep pages?"

### Luke Wroblewski — Conversion flow
- **Superpower:** conversion-oriented UX, mobile-first, forms, the mechanics of moving people to action.
- **Owns:** the CTA ladder, the path to "Book a Demo," form design, mobile experience, friction removal.
- **Always asks:** "What is the next action on this screen, and is it the easiest possible thing to do?"

### Edward Tufte — Information / proof design
- **Superpower:** presenting data and proof with maximum clarity and minimum clutter (high data-ink ratio).
- **Owns:** the stat/research sections, ROI visuals, comparison/convergence diagrams, proof presentation.
- **Always asks:** "Is every element earning its place, and does the proof read instantly and honestly?"

## What this group produces
- **User-flow maps:** how visitors enter and move through the site (per product, and the convergence path).
- **Wireframes** with **copy-slots** and **target word counts** per section (the contract the copywriting committee writes to).
- **Asset specs:** a consolidated list of *reused* assets + prioritized *net-new* assets (graphics, animations, mockups, diagrams) for Jeff to hand to the designer, with placement + intent.

## Collaboration with the copywriting committee
The UX group goes first (wireframe + slots + word counts), then convenes with the copywriting committee to walk the layout and explain what each slot must do. The copy committee drafts to those slots, returns for UX critique, revises, and the UX group signs off before it goes to Jeff. (Sequence detailed in `PRODUCTION_LOOP.md`.)

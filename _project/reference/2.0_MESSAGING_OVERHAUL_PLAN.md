## 1. The shift, in one paragraph

Move the site's top-of-funnel identity from a **product description** ("Peer-to-Peer Validation System" / "Peer Validation Platform for B2B Sales") to a **category** ("**The Trust Layer for B2B Sales**"). Pull **Carly** from a standalone, easily-missed page up into the homepage hero so she reads as the center of the product — matching how the deck frames her. Wrap both in the deck's **"why now"** narrative: AI makes information abundant, so trust becomes scarce, and SlashExperts/Carly delivers the right customer proof at the exact moment a buyer needs confidence to move. Keep the practical value props that already convert (the "invisible competition / 347 conversations" hook, deal acceleration, real customer proof) as the *proof* beneath the bigger category claim.

- **Positioning line lives in many places:** homepage `<title>`, meta description, JSON-LD, hero badge, the "What is SlashExperts" section, footer tagline, plus meta descriptions on ~12 other pages and a blog category. "Peer validation" is the dominant brand phrase site-wide (29+ "validation" occurrences).

- **Carly is absent from the homepage.** She only exists on a dedicated page (`src/pages/carly-ai-agent.njk`, "Carly — AI Social Proof on Autopilot"), pre-launch/"NEW" badge, early-access form, **no product imagery beyond one OG share image**.
- **AI is present but tactical** (ExpertScore AI matching, 50+ data points) — not yet a strategic "why now" story. **Trust** appears as a secondary theme (footer, blog category "The Science of Buyer Trust") but is not the headline.
- **Terminology debt:** `experts.njk` is titled "Expert **Directory**" — per current naming this should be "Expert **Booking System**."

## 3. Proposed messaging architecture

**Category line (new top-level positioning):** *The Trust Layer for B2B Sales.*

**Supporting one-liner (practical value prop, retained):** something close to the existing "Connect prospects with your happiest customers at the moments that matter most" — but reframed as *how the trust layer works*, not the category itself.

**Three narrative pillars (the "why now"), to thread through hero + a new section:**
1. **AI made information infinite.** Buyers can generate any answer instantly.
2. **So trust got scarce.** Synthetic content is everywhere; real proof from real customers is the only thing buyers believe.
3. **Carly delivers that proof at the decisive moment.** She watches active deals and routes the right customer proof/expert exactly when a buyer needs confidence to move.

> NOTE: Exact wording for the category line, hero, and pillars should mirror the deck's literal "one-liners." Those need to be confirmed (see Decision A) before final copy is written.
